Avoiding Churn

Published:

In Programming.

For around the past year, I have been sporadically working on writing my own C compiler. So far, I've struggled with two things--neither of which has anything to do with the C programming language: perfectionism and a lack of foresight. Right now there's around 4,800 lines of Rust code (excluding empty lines or comments) and around 1,000 lines of test cases. This is after 90 commits with a total of nearly 14,000 lines inserted and 4,000 lines deleted1.

  1. Easily calculated by pasting the output of git log --numstat --pretty="" into a spread sheet. ↩
